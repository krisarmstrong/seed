## 2. Marketing Channels & Tactics

### 2.1 Product-Led Growth (Freemium)

**Strategy:**
Offer a generous free tier (50 devices, basic monitoring) to drive adoption, then upsell to paid tiers.

**Tactics:**
1. **Free Tier Features:**
   - Network discovery (50 devices)
   - Basic monitoring (link status, ping)
   - WiFi scanning
   - 7-day metric retention

2. **Upgrade Triggers:**
   - Hit 50-device limit → "Upgrade to Starter for 200 devices"
   - View AI insights (grayed out) → "Upgrade to Pro for root cause analysis"
   - Try predictive survey (blocked) → "Upgrade to Premium for WiFi planning"

3. **In-App Messaging:**
   - NPS surveys (identify promoters)
   - Feature announcements (drive upgrades)
   - Usage tips ("Did you know...?")

4. **Viral Loops:**
   - "Invite colleague" (share dashboard view-only access)
   - Exportable reports with "Generated by The Seed" watermark
   - Public case studies (with customer permission)

**Metrics:**
- Free signups: 500+ Month 1 → 2,000+ Month 12
- Free-to-Paid conversion: 15%+ (75-300 paying customers Year 1)
- Time to upgrade: <30 days avg

**Budget:** $0 (product is the marketing)

## 5. Brand Guidelines & Messaging

### 5.1 Brand Voice & Tone

**Brand Personality:**
- **Intelligent:** We're technical experts, not sales fluff
- **Approachable:** We explain complex things simply
- **Trustworthy:** We're honest about what we can/can't do
- **Proactive:** We prevent problems, not just react
- **Healthcare-Focused:** We understand your unique challenges

**Tone:**
- **Professional but not stiff:** "Here's how it works" not "Please be advised..."
- **Confident but not arrogant:** "We're the best at X" not "We're the best at everything"
- **Helpful but not condescending:** "Here's a tip" not "Obviously, you should..."

**Do's:**
- ✅ Use "you" and "your" (customer-centric)
- ✅ Use active voice ("The Seed detects..." not "Devices are detected by...")
- ✅ Use specific numbers ("Save 15 hours/week" not "Save time")
- ✅ Explain benefits, not just features ("So you can focus on projects" not "Has AI")

**Don'ts:**
- ❌ Buzzword bingo ("Synergize your network paradigm")
- ❌ Vague claims ("Best in class", "Industry-leading")
- ❌ Technical jargon without explanation (to non-engineers)
- ❌ Negative competitor comparisons ("SolarWinds sucks")

## 9. Crisis Management & Brand Protection

### Handling Negative Feedback

**Scenario: Customer Complains on Social Media**

**Bad Response:**
❌ "That's not true. Our product works fine."

**Good Response:**
✅ "We're sorry to hear you had issues. DM us your email and we'll get this fixed ASAP. We take customer feedback seriously."

**Follow-up:**
- Resolve issue privately (email/call)
- Offer refund or credit if appropriate
- Ask permission to follow up publicly: "We've resolved [Name]'s issue. Thanks for the feedback!"

---

**Scenario: Competitor Claims We're "Not Enterprise-Ready"**

**Bad Response:**
❌ "They're just scared of us. We're better than them."

**Good Response:**
✅ Ignore (don't feed the trolls). Focus on customer success stories.

**Alternative (if pressed):**
✅ "We're focused on healthcare and SMB where we deliver incredible value. Enterprise is on our roadmap for 2027."
